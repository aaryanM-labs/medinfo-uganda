About this project

Built by a student.
Designed for Uganda.

Most online symptom checkers are designed for Western countries, listing conditions that rarely exist in Uganda while missing the diseases that actually affect people here every day.

🎯

The Problem

When someone in Uganda searches their symptoms online, they get results built for patients in the UK or USA. Diseases like malaria, typhoid, bilharzia, and sleeping sickness are either missing or buried. People make wrong decisions because the tools available do not reflect their reality.

💡

The Solution

MedInfo Uganda is a symptom awareness tool built specifically around diseases common in Uganda. Every condition in the database was researched and written using biology knowledge — not copied from generic sources. The science knowledge is the product. The code is the delivery mechanism.

🔬

What I Learned

The hardest part of building this was not the code — it was the biology. Any programmer can build a search function. But accurately distinguishing how malaria presents clinically versus typhoid or meningitis requires scientific understanding. This project showed me that bioinformatics — combining biology with technology — is where I want to build my career.

Who built this

I am a Biology, Chemistry and Mathematics A-Level student in Uganda finishing my studies in 2027. I built MedInfo Uganda during my S5 year as a personal project to apply my science knowledge to a real problem in my community.

My goal is to study Bioinformatics at university — using computational methods to understand diseases at a molecular level and contribute to research on diagnostics and treatments relevant to Africa.

This project is the first step in that direction.
